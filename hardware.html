Hardware

Motherboard

The motherboard brings all parts of a computer together. It is how the different parts communicate to each other.

CPU

The CPU stands for Central Processing Unit. It is well known as the “brains” or intellect of a computer. It takes care of the information or directions of a computer. It basically controls everything in your computer.

RAM

RAM stands for random access memory. It is the memory or space for your computer. RAM controls how fast your computer will run. If you have more RAM then you can have more applications and it will run faster.

Hard Drive

A hard drive is the storage device for your computer. It is where your files are saved to and it stores them.

Communication of Components

All the different components communicate with one another through the computer bus. A computer bus includes different materialistic networks such as; wires, cables, etc. It is composed of three contrasting subassemblies including the data bus (conveys the guidance of the processor), address bus (transits recollection addresses, which allows the processor to understand data) , and control bus (shifts the signals from the management unit). Also, the leading buses are formulated by the internal and expansion bus. The North and South bridge form the chipset, which programs the statistics among the computer bus. This way the components of a computer are able to interact with one another.

Keyboard, Mouse,+ Monitor

The keyboard is where you type on your computer. It contains letters, numbers, symbols, etc. It is how you would control what you type on your computer. A mouse moves the cursor of your computer around your screen. It is how you click and transport different objects on your screen. A monitor exhibits what you see on your screen (pictures,videos,etc.)
